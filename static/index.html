Exercise Movement Mappings

Exercises are pulled from your Hevy workout history and auto-classified by keyword rules into one of four movement patterns: Quad, Hip, Push, and Pull. Blended movements like a conventional deadlift can be split across multiple patterns (e.g. 20% Quad / 80% Hip).

Important — what the percentages do and don't do: The pattern split has no effect on the fatigue numbers themselves. Your total central and peripheral stress for a session is calculated entirely from RPE and reps and will be identical regardless of how an exercise is classified. The split only controls which bucket on the pattern chart receives the credit — it's a planning lens for tracking volume balance across movement families, not a fatigue predictor.

The actual fatigue signal comes from logging RPE honestly. A knee-level rack pull and a deficit deadlift logged at the same RPE will produce the same stress score — if the deficit pull felt harder, log it at a higher RPE and the system will reflect that automatically. The daily check-in catches whatever the numbers miss.

To review an exercise: click Edit, confirm or adjust the split, then click Save & Mark Reviewed. Auto-classified exercises show unreviewed until confirmed. Use the Unreviewed only filter to work through the list efficiently. If a movement should not count toward stress at all, enable Exclude movement from stress calculations in the editor.
